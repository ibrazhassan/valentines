For fourteen fortnights more,

For the journey that is bright with light divine.

And let the stars bear witness, solemnly they bow, and tides start rushing to the shore.

For fate has whispered: Together shall we shine.


And the winter's hush, I see the birth of the falling snow,

With each flake, a whisper, so delicate and rare.

Yet none so perfect, none so bright,

as though, it's thou who hath they long to mirror the grace.

Yet match thee not, for thou art past compare...

They trace thy elegance in frozen lace.


Vignettes of gardens, we envisioned, they'd bloom as time would weave,

Yet in thoughts of mine, truth, so wondrous it'd grow,

Thou art the garden, ineffable, it be,

how my soul hath been set aglow.


And in the quiet of my heart, I find the truth unveiled,

That thou art all I ever sought, though unaware, I roamed,

Thy laughter, a melody that stirs my very core,

Thy gaze, a harbor where my restless soul finds home.

Thou hast shown me joy in its purest form,

A happiness so vast, so rare, it seems a dream.

How could one so perfect, so wondrously kind,

Exist beyond the bounds of my most fervent hope?

Yet here thou art, my miracle in flesh and grace,

And with thee, eternity feels but a fleeting trace.


I dream of days where silver lines our hair,

When time has etched its story on our skin.

To build a life, a tapestry of memories,

Where dreams, once whispered, now are woven in.

Hand in hand, through sunlit fields and stormy skies,

With thee, each moment, a treasure unsurpassed.


Thus, let the seasons wane and moments soar,

Till love doth reign,

For fourteen fortnights more.
